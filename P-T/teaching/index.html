Teaching Portfolio (2001-2007)

by Abhik Roychoudhury

 

Background Information

My teaching and research interests are in formal models and methods for reliable system design. In the last three years, I have taught two courses in this area CS4271 and CS6214. Out of these, CS4271 is an undergraduate elective course in our Computer Engineering program. The average enrolment in this course in the last three years stands at (approximately) 45 students. CS6214 is a specialized graduate course studying theoretically advanced methods for reasoning about software properties. The average enrolment of this course is (approximately) 12 students.

In both of these courses, I have strived for integration of teaching and research. In the graduate course, I have been encouraged by feedback from some students to conduct new research and the topics of this new research have been fed back to later offerings of the course. In the undergraduate course (which focuses on formal validation of embedded systems), I have used case studies conducted in my research to provide classroom exercises for discussing application of the validation techniques. Also, the students in this undergraduate course have successfully taken up term projects which try to solve challenge problems proposed in the verification research community (as evidenced in the sample project in the CS4271 module folder).

G. Article on Education/Pedagogy Authored
