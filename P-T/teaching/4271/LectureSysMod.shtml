Sample Lecture on System Modeling

This lecture discusses the use of practical modeling tools for modeling behaviors of reactive systems. The lecture is centered around the simplifications that can be employed by a system designer to model real-life systems. These simplifications have been summarized in the second last slide of the lecture.

The lecture uses a modeling and model checking tool called Symbolic Model Verifier (SMV). SMV has been widely used for high-level modeling and debugging of hardware as well as mixed hardware-software designs.

In the lecture, we use a concrete example of a cache coherence protocol to elaborate the modeling steps. The case study is chosen carefully:  not too simple to be trivial, not too complex to comprehend in the span of a lecture. It helps the students to understand the system modeling principles in a classroom setting.
